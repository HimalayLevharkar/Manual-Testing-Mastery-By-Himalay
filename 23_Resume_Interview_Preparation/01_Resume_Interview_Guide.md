### ✅ Resume Writing Tips

```
DO's:
✓ Use action verbs: Created, Identified, Improved, Designed
✓ Quantify achievements: "50+ test cases", "45 bugs", "95% coverage"
✓ Use simple language, easy to read
✓ Focus on impact: What did you achieve?
✓ ATS-friendly format (no fancy formatting)
✓ Keep to 1 page (max 1.5 for experienced)
✓ Use industry keywords: JIRA, Agile, BVA, ECP, etc.
✓ Show continuous learning: Certifications, trainings

DON'Ts:
✗ Personal details: photo, marital status, age
✗ Spelling/grammar mistakes (shows attention to detail)
✗ Irrelevant information: Hobbies (unless relevant)
✗ Long paragraphs (use bullet points)
✗ Vague achievements: "Worked on testing" (be specific)
✗ Lying about experience or certifications
✗ More than 1.5 pages (keep it concise)
✗ Fancy formatting that doesn't render in ATS
```

### 📋 Resume Checklist

```
□ Contact information clear and correct?
□ Professional summary compelling?
□ All skills relevant to job posting?
□ Experience listed in reverse chronological order?
□ Achievements quantified with numbers?
□ Action verbs used throughout?
□ No spelling or grammar errors?
□ 1-1.5 pages max?
□ ATS-friendly format (no tables, colors)?
□ Keywords from job description included?
□ Email professional (not yahoo123)?
□ Phone number current and active?
□ Formatting consistent (bullets, dates)?
```

## <a name="portfolio"></a>Portfolio Building

### 🎯 Creating a Testing Portfolio

```
What to Include:

1. SAMPLE TEST CASES
   - 10-20 well-written test cases
   - Show variety: positive, negative, boundary
   - Use professional format
   - Include test data, expected results
   
   Example:
   - E-commerce: Login, Search, Add to Cart, Checkout
   - Banking: Money Transfer, Bill Payment, Account Update
   - Social Media: Post Creation, Image Upload, Comments

2. BUG REPORTS
   - 5-10 well-documented bug reports
   - Show good STR, clear expected vs actual
   - Include screenshots
   - Show different severity levels
   - Show good communication

3. TEST PLANS & DOCUMENTATION
   - Sample test plan for a project
   - Test strategy documents
   - RTM (Requirements Traceability Matrix)
   - Show understanding of project

4. PROCESS IMPROVEMENTS
   - How did you improve testing process?
   - Reduced cycle time?
   - Created templates?
   - Improved documentation?
   - Trained others?

5. GITHUB PROFILE
   - Share test automation scripts (if any)
   - Test data templates
   - Useful scripts/tools
   - Shows you're tech-savvy

6. BLOG/MEDIUM ARTICLES
   - Write testing tips: "How to write good test cases"
   - "BVA and ECP explained with examples"
   - "Common bugs in e-commerce apps"
   - Shows communication skills

7. VIDEO DEMONSTRATIONS
   - Screen recording of testing a real app
   - Narrate your process
   - Show exploratory testing
   - Upload to YouTube or drive

WHERE TO SHARE:
- GitHub (code, templates)
- Medium/Dev.to (articles)
- LinkedIn (projects, articles)
- Your own blog
- Google Drive (shared link in LinkedIn)
- Notion page (professional portfolio)
```

## <a name="interview"></a>Interview Preparation

### 📚 Pre-Interview Checklist

```
1 WEEK BEFORE:

□ Research the company
  - What products/services?
  - What's their tech stack?
  - Recent news or funding?
  - Who are competitors?
  - What's their culture?
  - LinkedIn check on interviewers

□ Review the job description
  - What skills are they looking for?
  - What projects might I work on?
  - What tools do they use?
  - What are must-haves vs nice-to-haves?

□ Prepare stories
  - 3-5 real project stories
  - Use STAR method
  - Show your skills/values

□ Practice common questions
  - Technical questions (20+)
  - Behavioral questions (10+)
  - Scenario questions (10+)
  - Ask them questions (5+)

□ Technical preparation
  - Brush up on testing concepts
  - Review SDLC, STLC
  - Review testing types
  - Know tool basics (JIRA, Postman)

1 DAY BEFORE:

□ Test your setup (if virtual)
  - Camera working?
  - Microphone working?
  - Internet stable?
  - Background appropriate?
  - Lighting good?

□ Prepare materials
  - Print resume (hard copy)
  - Portfolio on phone/laptop
  - List of questions for them
  - Notepad and pen

□ Get good sleep
□ Prepare outfit
□ Know directions (if in-person)
□ Plan travel time (arrive 15 min early)

DAY OF INTERVIEW:

□ Wake up early
□ Have breakfast/coffee
□ Review your talking points
□ Calm your nerves
□ Be confident
□ Smile :)
```

### 🎤 Practice Your Delivery

```
Mock Interview Process:

1. WITH FRIEND/COLLEAGUE
   - Ask them to interview you
   - Practice your answers
   - Get feedback
   - Record if possible

2. WITH ONLINE RESOURCES
   - Use YouTube videos
   - Practice with AI interview prep
   - Record yourself answering
   - Review your tone, pace, clarity

3. FOCUS AREAS
   - Speak clearly, not too fast
   - Make eye contact (even via video)
   - Show enthusiasm and energy
   - Smile while speaking
   - Use hand gestures (naturally)
   - Don't use filler words (um, uh, like)
   - Pause between thoughts (not awkward silence)
   - Active listening (nod, take notes)
   - Ask clarifying questions if needed
```

## <a name="day-of-interview"></a>Day of Interview

### 🎯 Do's on Interview Day

```
✓ DO:

1. ARRIVE EARLY
   - 15 minutes before
   - Find the conference room
   - Calm your nerves
   - Use bathroom before
   - Check your appearance

2. DRESS PROFESSIONALLY
   - Business formal or business casual
   - Clean, ironed clothes
   - Neat hair and grooming
   - Closed-toe shoes
   - Minimal jewelry
   - No heavy perfume/cologne

3. GREETING
   - Firm handshake
   - Smile
   - Eye contact
   - "Nice to meet you"
   - Ask their name and role

4. LISTENING
   - Listen to full question
   - Don't interrupt
   - Take notes
   - Ask clarification if needed
   - Show interest in their situation

5. ANSWERING
   - Answer the question asked
   - Be specific with examples
   - Use STAR method
   - Back up claims with proof
   - Show confidence
   - Be honest

6. ENGAGEMENT
   - Ask thoughtful questions
   - Show you researched company
   - Show enthusiasm about role
   - Show interest in their team
   - Take notes (shows interest)

7. CLOSING
   - Recap your interest
   - Ask about next steps
   - Exchange contact info
   - Thank them for time
   - Confident goodbye
```

### ❌ Don'ts on Interview Day

```
✗ DON'T:

1. BE LATE
   - Traffic, delays happen
   - Leave extra early
   - Being late shows disrespect

2. DRESS INAPPROPRIATELY
   - Too casual (hoodies, shorts)
   - Too formal (3-piece suit, unless banking)
   - Torn, stained, wrinkled clothes
   - Visible tattoos/piercings (unless company culture)

3. POOR BODY LANGUAGE
   - Slouching
   - Fidgeting
   - Not making eye contact
   - Crossing arms
   - Negative expression
   - Looking at phone

4. INTERRUPT
   - Let them finish
   - Even if you know the answer
   - Even if you disagree

5. COMPLAIN
   - Don't criticize old employer
   - Don't complain about salary
   - Don't complain about work
   - Stay positive always

6. LIE
   - "I know Java" (when you don't)
   - "I have AWS experience" (when you don't)
   - You'll get caught
   - Honesty is better

7. OVERSHARE
   - Personal problems
   - Family issues
   - Health conditions
   - Political views
   - Religious beliefs
   Keep professional

8. RUSH
   - Slow down your speech
   - Don't speak too fast
   - Think before answering
   - Pause is ok (not long pauses)
```

### 📱 Virtual Interview Tips

```
ADDITIONAL TIPS FOR VIDEO INTERVIEWS:

1. CAMERA
   - Eye level
   - Good lighting (face well-lit)
   - No backlight (window behind)
   - Webcam clean
   - Framing: head and shoulders visible
   - Test video quality before

2. BACKGROUND
   - Clean, professional
   - Not cluttered
   - Neutral color (blue, white, grey)
   - No distracting items
   - Virtual background if needed (test it)

3. SOUND
   - Microphone working well
   - Test audio beforehand
   - Minimize background noise
   - Close doors to reduce echo
   - Wear headphones for better clarity

4. INTERNET
   - Use wired connection (more stable)
   - If WiFi, position near router
   - Close unnecessary apps/downloads
   - Disconnect other devices
   - Have backup (phone hotspot)

5. MATERIALS
   - Resume printed (visible to you)
   - Notes in front of you (they won't see)
   - Pen and paper
   - Drink of water nearby
   - No food/eating

6. TIMING
   - Join 5 minutes early
   - Check everything working
   - Take a breath, relax
   - Smile when they join (shows on video)
```

### 💭 Email Timing & Follow-Up

```
TIMELINE:

After Interview:
- Send thank you email within 24 hours
- Reference specific discussion points
- Reiterate interest
- Ask about timeline

After 1 week:
- If no response, follow up politely
- "I'm still very interested in the position"
- "Do you need any additional information?"
- Keep it brief and professional

After 2 weeks:
- One more follow-up
- If still no response, move on
- They might be interviewing others

If you get an offer:
- Express gratitude
- Don't immediately accept or reject
- Take 24-48 hours to decide
- Negotiate if needed
- Get offer in writing

If you don't get the job:
- Ask for feedback
- Thank them for opportunity
- Stay in touch (for future roles)
- Learn from experience
```

### 🤝 Negotiation Tips

```
DO'S:

✓ Know your value
  - Research market rates
  - Know your skills level
  - Have supporting examples

✓ Ask for range
  "Based on my experience in e-commerce testing, 
   my expected range is 8-10 LPA"

✓ Back it with reasons
  "I have 3 years of experience, ISTQB certification,
   and have consistently found critical bugs"

✓ Negotiate professionally
  "Thank you for the offer of 7 LPA. Given my 
   skills and experience, I was expecting 8-9 LPA. 
   Could we negotiate?"

✓ Get everything in writing
  - Salary
  - Benefits
  - Joining date
  - Designation
  - Reporting manager

✓ Consider total package
  - Not just salary
  - Benefits, growth, learning important
  - Work-life balance
  - Company culture


DON'Ts:

✗ Demand unreasonable amount
  - Research first
  - Be realistic

✗ Give a low number
  - They won't increase
  - Always give a range

✗ Be aggressive
  - Professional tone
  - "I need Rs. X or I'm out" - bad approach

✗ Accept immediately
  - Take time to think
  - Get offer in writing
  - Compare options

✗ Lie about other offers
  - Can be verified
  - Ruins trust

✗ Only focus on salary
  - Growth, learning matter
  - Work-life balance matters
  - Company culture matters


NEGOTIATION SCRIPT:

Company: "We'd like to offer you 7 LPA"

You: "Thank you for the offer. I'm excited about 
      the opportunity to work with your team.

      Based on my 3 years of testing experience, 
      ISTQB certification, and my track record of 
      finding critical bugs, I was expecting a salary 
      in the range of 8-9 LPA.

      Is there room for adjustment?"

Company: "We can offer 7.5 LPA"

You: "Can we also discuss additional benefits? 
      Perhaps work-from-home flexibility or learning 
      budget for certifications?"

Then decide if acceptable.
```
